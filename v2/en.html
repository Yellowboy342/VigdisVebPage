The schedule

Each day, made of pictures

Build a daily plan from images and icons your family already recognises. Tasks appear in order — morning, afternoon, evening — so no one has to ask what's next.

Flexible for school transitions, weekends, evening routines, and the days that don't go to plan.

Breakdowns

Big tasks become small ones

"Get dressed" becomes — Socks. Pants. Shirt. Each step ticked off as it's done. The child sees what's left; you don't have to stand there reminding.

Socks Pants Shirt

The week, at a glance

See the pattern — not a grade

A weekly overview shows where routines are working and where they're falling apart. No grades, no warnings. Just enough to know where to put more support next week.

Rewards

Stars that mean something

Points earned across daily routines can become the things your family already values — a movie night, an ice-cream trip, extra screen time. You decide what's on the list and what each one costs.

Privacy

On the device — not in the cloud

Vigdís works locally. What you create stays on your iPhone or iPad — not on a server somewhere. No ads, no data collection, no cross-app tracking.

On-device only Family routines stay where they belong.
No data collection No accounts required to use the app.
Works offline No network, subscription, or signup needed to use it.

Cloud sync is optional — for families who want to share a profile across devices or with another parent. You turn it on when you need it, and off when you don't.

Who built Vigdís

Built by people
who use it daily

Vigdís started as a personal tool. A special-education professional and parent with over 10 years of experience designing visual schedules for children with complex support needs went looking for an app that fit — and didn't find one. So we built the one we wanted.

It's still designed that way. No complicated sign-ups, no menus you have to figure out, and nothing competing for the children's attention.

Vigdís is named after Vigdís Finnbogadóttir — the world's first democratically elected female president. She was also a teacher.
